Pythagorean Theorem

The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right triangle:

\[a^2 + b^2 = c^2\]

Where:

  • \(a\) and \(b\) are the lengths of the two legs (the sides adjacent to the right angle)
  • \(c\) is the length of the hypotenuse (the longest side, opposite to the right angle)

Example:

If we have a right triangle with:

  • leg \(a = 3\) units
  • leg \(b = 4\) units

Then the hypotenuse \(c\) can be calculated as:

\[c^2 = 3^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25\] \[c = \sqrt{25} = 5\text{ units}\]
